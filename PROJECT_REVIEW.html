Full title: "Discourse around Sleep, Performance, and Work in the U.S. Print Media: 1983-2017"

Key Arguments

  • Work vs. sleep tension — Sleep framed as sacrificed for productivity; exhaustion as "badge of honor"
  • Incomplete customization — Sleep reframed as performance enhancer, but napping still stigmatized (70% nap secretly at work)
  • Protestant ethic of sleep — Traces ambivalence to Franklin's maxims and Puritan morality
  • Medicalization & healthicization — Sleep increasingly framed as health obligation and medical concern
